07 Assumptions & Notes

Method & assumptions

  1. Method follows Niu, Airframe Stress Analysis & Sizing, Section 6.5 — load redistribution around a skin cut-out using fixed-end-moment frame idealisation. [SCT-025]
  2. Door capability factors ηh / ηl default to 0 = full Niu redistribution (the plug door transfers all hoop / longitudinal load to the surrounding frames & sills). Set a non-zero value only with a substantiated door load path — ηh > 0 reduces the redistributed frame load and is unconservative without justification. [deviates from Niu §6.5]
  3. General bays (Niu §6.5): upper a, lower b, forward c, aft d are independent. Corner shears, edge redistribution and member loads are evaluated per location and the governing value is reported. Default geometry reproduces Niu's worked example (adjusted corner moment 169,198 in·lb; upper-fwd corner 210.2 lbf/in; q₀=450 → q₁/q₂/q₃ = 1108/932/255). Headline values are the governing (worst) location.
  4. Section properties — neutral-axis offset ȳ and second moment of area I must be derived separately from the built-up frame + bear-strap + skin section. Enter the computed values.
  5. Worst-case frame hoop load takes the greater of the stop-fitting load and the cut-out redistribution load. If the stop-fitting load is the lesser, stop loads are insufficient to react frame tension and the frame pulls inward against the door until stop-fitting loads rise to match frame tension (free-edge equilibrium).
  6. Inner-chord stress is lower than nominal hoop due to bending compression, but this is localised at the frame ends; the nominal hoop stress is retained as the conservative peak value.
  7. Body shear (Case I) — the fuselage skin shear flow is diverted around the cut-out per Niu §6.5. Enter four band shear flows: qa (upper aux), qb (upper main), qc (lower main), qd (lower aux). Equal bands → uniform shear (cut-out on the neutral axis, Fig 6.5.3/6.5.4) — validated against Niu's worked example (450 → q₁/q₂/q₃ = 1108/932/255 lbf/in). Unequal bands → variable shear for off-NA cut-outs (floor / cargo, Fig 6.5.6–6.5.8): the cut-out height splits hb=a·h/(a+b), hc=b·h/(a+b) and the upper/lower panels redistribute their own band shear. Headline checks use the governing (worst) panel. [Niu §6.5 Case I]
  8. Junction shear — per Niu, strap/skin/doubler areas at the main-sill/edge-frame junctions are checked at 5/3 · qav for local load concentration / fatigue life, and the panel gross-area shear stress is kept ≤ ~25 ksi (Al).
  9. Case I frame loads (Fig 6.5.9) — the edge-frame web shear is distributed 3:1 across the upper/lower bays (3:1:1:3 across the mid bay); the peak frame shear flow at the main-sill junctions (3·½(q₁+q₃)) is exact and reproduces Niu's 2045 lbf/in. The sill reactions REF/RGH from the curved-frame radial-resultant equilibrium are preliminary: the solve is ill-conditioned (large cancelling terms) and sensitive to the cut-out's position on the arc (assumed centred on the neutral axis), reproducing Niu within ~5–8%. Detailed frame bending should be taken to conventional shear/BM diagrams or FEM once these reactions are set, per Niu.
  10. Not yet included: the variable-shear option for off-neutral-axis cut-outs (Fig 6.5.6–6.5.8) and combination of shear with pressure per Niu's design conditions (3.0p; 2.5p; flight+2.0p; flight).
